STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE: SPRAY DRYER CRITICAL CONTROL POINTS
Document: dryer_sop.txt | Version: 1.0 | Effective: 2026-01-01
Applicable Standard: FDA 21 CFR Part 110, ISO 22000:2018

§1 Normal Operation
The spray dryer outlet temperature must be maintained between 100°C and 130°C to ensure
microbial reduction targets are met while preventing protein degradation. Outlet humidity
(relative humidity of the drying chamber exhaust) must not exceed 40% RH. Both parameters
are monitored continuously. The inlet temperature setpoint is 185°C; outlet temperature is
controlled by adjusting feed rate and inlet temperature in combination.

§2 Temperature Deviation
§2.1 Low Outlet Temperature (Below 100°C)
Low outlet temperature indicates insufficient drying, leaving product with elevated moisture
content that supports microbial growth. Immediate action: increase inlet temperature setpoint
by 5°C increments and reduce feed rate by 10%. If outlet temperature cannot be restored
within 10 minutes, stop feed and place all collected powder from the deviation window on
hold. Product moisture content must be verified before release. Document per §5.

§2.2 High Outlet Temperature (Above 130°C)
Overheating causes Maillard browning, lysine degradation, and thermal damage to heat-labile
nutrients. Reduce inlet temperature setpoint by 5°C increments and increase feed rate by
5%. If temperature exceeds 140°C, halt production and investigate inlet temperature
controller. Affected powder must be assessed for colour and nutrient content by QA.

§3 Humidity Exceedance (Above 40% RH)
§3.1 Elevated outlet humidity indicates incomplete moisture removal. This creates conditions
for powder caking, microbial growth, and reduced shelf life. Check cyclone separator for
blockage. Verify dehumidification system operation. Reduce feed rate by 15%.

§3.2 If humidity remains above 45% RH for more than 5 minutes, stop production. Inspect
drying chamber for wet-wall deposition. Powder collected during the exceedance period must
be quarantined and tested for water activity (target: aw ≤ 0.25) before any release decision.

§4 Product Hold Procedure
§4.1 All powder collected during a CCP deviation window must be bagged separately, labelled
with batch ID, deviation type, start and end time, and stored in the quarantine area.
§4.2 Release of quarantined powder requires: (a) documented root cause, (b) corrective action
record, (c) QA sign-off, (d) verification testing results (moisture, microbiology if required).
