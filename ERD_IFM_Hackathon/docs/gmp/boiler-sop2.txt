Boiler Operations SOP
Infant Food Manufacturing Plant – IoT Monitoring Simulation
Equipment: Steam Boiler System
Machine Type: boiler
Monitoring Frequency: Every 5 seconds via IoT telemetry
1. Purpose
This SOP defines the actions operators must take when the boiler IoT monitoring system detects abnormal operating conditions beyond acceptable safety thresholds.

The SOP is intended for Boiler Operators, Shift Supervisors, Maintenance Teams, and Process Engineers.
2. Process Importance
The boiler provides steam required for ingredient sterilization, spray drying systems, CIP operations, heating, and pasteurization.

Abnormal boiler conditions can result in product contamination risk, steam supply interruption, equipment damage, operator safety hazards, and production downtime.
3. IoT Parameters Monitored
Steam Temperature: 180°C – 250°C | Abnormal: > 280°C
Pressure: 8 – 18 bar | Abnormal: > 20 bar
Water Level: 55% – 85% | Abnormal: < 50%
Fuel Flow Rate: 80 – 160 L/hr
4. Alarm Severity Levels
Warning: Parameter approaching limit
Critical: Threshold exceeded
Emergency: Persistent abnormality > 2 minutes
5. SOP for High Steam Temperature
Condition: Steam Temperature > 280°C

Immediate Actions:
- Acknowledge alarm on monitoring dashboard
- Reduce burner firing rate by 10–20%
- Verify steam outlet valves are open
- Check burner chamber behavior
- Confirm temperature sensor operation

Emergency Condition:
If temperature exceeds 320°C, initiate controlled shutdown.
6. SOP for High Boiler Pressure
Condition: Pressure > 20 bar

Immediate Actions:
- Reduce burner load
- Verify pressure relief valve operation
- Inspect steam lines for blockage

Emergency Condition:
If pressure exceeds 25 bar, perform emergency shutdown and evacuate non-essential personnel.
7. SOP for Low Water Level
Condition: Water Level < 50%

Immediate Actions:
- Verify feed water pump operation
- Check inlet valve status
- Inspect for leaks

Emergency Condition:
If water level drops below 30%, stop burner operation immediately.
8. Boiler Shutdown Procedure
1. Reduce burner load gradually
2. Stop fuel supply
3. Maintain water circulation
4. Vent excess steam safely
5. Log shutdown reason
9. Notification & Escalation Matrix
Warning Alarm → Shift Operator
Critical Alarm → Shift Supervisor
Emergency Shutdown → Plant Manager + Safety Team
Repeated Alarms → Maintenance Engineering
10. Operator Logging Requirements
Operators must record timestamp of alarm, sensor value, corrective actions taken, duration of abnormal condition, and maintenance ticket number.

12. Recommended Dashboard Status Logic
Healthy → All parameters within range
Warning → Near threshold
Critical → Threshold exceeded
Shutdown Required → Severe abnormality
13. Simulation Notes
Abnormal conditions occur randomly every 3–10 minutes.
The monitoring dashboard should display alerts, SOP guidance, maintenance ticket creation, and shutdown simulation workflows.
