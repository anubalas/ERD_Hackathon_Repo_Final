Evaporator Operations SOP
Infant Food Manufacturing Plant – IoT Monitoring Simulation
Equipment: Multi-Effect Evaporator System
Machine Type: evaporator
Monitoring Frequency: Every 5 seconds via IoT telemetry
1. Purpose
This SOP defines operational response procedures for abnormal evaporator conditions detected through the IoT monitoring system.
2. Process Overview
The evaporator removes excess water from liquid food slurry before spray drying and helps achieve the required solids concentration while preserving nutritional quality.
3. Critical Role in Infant Food Manufacturing
Improper evaporator operation may lead to burnt product, nutrient degradation, fouling, energy loss, batch rejection, and production downtime.
4. IoT Parameters Monitored
Steam Temperature: 85°C – 105°C | Abnormal: > 110°C
Pressure: 1.8 – 2.8 bar | Abnormal: > 3.0 bar
Liquid Level: 40% – 85% | Abnormal: < 30%
5. Sensor Definitions
Steam Temperature controls evaporation rate and protects nutrients.
Pressure maintains stable boiling conditions.
Liquid Level prevents dry heating and tube damage.
6. Alarm Severity Classification
Advisory → Trending toward limit
Warning → Near threshold
Critical → Threshold exceeded
Emergency → Shutdown required
7. SOP – High Steam Temperature
Condition: Steam Temperature > 110°C

Immediate Actions:
- Acknowledge alarm
- Verify sensor reading
- Reduce steam input
- Check feed flow
- Inspect condensate removal

Emergency Shutdown if temperature exceeds 125°C.
8. SOP – High Pressure
Condition: Pressure > 3.0 bar

Immediate Actions:
- Reduce steam supply
- Inspect vapor outlet
- Verify vacuum system
- Check safety relief devices

Emergency Shutdown if pressure exceeds 4.0 bar.
9. SOP – Low Liquid Level
Condition: Liquid Level < 30%

Immediate Actions:
- Verify feed system
- Reduce steam heating
- Inspect for leaks
- Confirm sensor validity

Emergency Shutdown if level drops below 15%.
10. Controlled Shutdown Procedure
1. Stop product feed gradually
2. Reduce steam supply slowly
3. Normalize pressure
4. Drain product safely
5. Flush evaporator
6. Isolate steam supply
11. Product Quality Protection Procedure
If abnormal conditions persist, place affected batch on HOLD and notify QA department.
12. Maintenance Inspection Checklist
Inspect steam valves, sensors, feed pumps, vacuum system, condensers, relief valves, and tube fouling condition.
13. Operator Logging Requirements
Operators must document alarm type, timestamp, readings, corrective actions, escalation details, and shutdown actions.
14. Escalation Matrix
Warning Alarm → Shift Operator
Critical Alarm → Shift Supervisor
Repeated Alarm → Maintenance Team
Quality Risk → QA Department
Emergency Shutdown → Plant Manager + Safety Team
