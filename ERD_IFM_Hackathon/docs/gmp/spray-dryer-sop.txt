Spray Dryer Operations SOP
Infant Food Manufacturing Plant – IoT Monitoring Simulation
Equipment: Industrial Spray Dryer System
Machine Type: spray_dryer
Monitoring Frequency: Every 5 seconds via IoT telemetry
1. Purpose
This SOP defines operational response procedures for abnormal spray dryer conditions detected through the IoT monitoring system.
2. Process Overview
The spray dryer converts concentrated liquid infant food slurry into dry powder using heated air and atomization technology.
3. Importance in Infant Food Manufacturing
Improper spray drying may lead to excess moisture, nutrient degradation, powder lumping, microbial risks, and fire hazards.
4. IoT Parameters Monitored
Inlet Temperature: 160°C – 195°C | Abnormal: > 200°C
Outlet Temperature: 85°C – 110°C | Abnormal: < 80°C or > 120°C
Humidity: 6% – 14% | Abnormal: > 15%
5. Parameter Definitions
Inlet Temperature controls drying energy.
Outlet Temperature indicates drying efficiency.
Humidity indicates final powder moisture conditions.
6. Alarm Severity Levels
Advisory → Trending abnormal
Warning → Near threshold
Critical → Threshold exceeded
Emergency → Unsafe condition
7. SOP – High Inlet Temperature
Condition: Inlet Temperature > 200°C

Immediate Actions:
- Reduce burner firing rate
- Inspect airflow system
- Verify temperature sensors
- Check product feed stability

Emergency shutdown if temperature exceeds 230°C.
8. SOP – High Humidity
Condition: Humidity > 15%

Immediate Actions:
- Verify heating performance
- Reduce slurry feed rate
- Inspect atomizer and exhaust airflow

Shutdown if humidity exceeds 25%.
9. SOP – High Outlet Temperature
Condition: Outlet Temperature > 120°C

Immediate Actions:
- Reduce inlet heat
- Increase feed rate slightly
- Verify airflow stability

Shutdown if outlet temperature exceeds 140°C.
10. SOP – Low Outlet Temperature
Condition: Outlet Temperature < 80°C

Immediate Actions:
- Increase drying temperature
- Reduce feed rate
- Inspect atomizer and exhaust system

Shutdown if outlet temperature falls below 60°C.
11. Powder Quality Protection Procedure
Hold affected powder lots, notify QA, perform moisture analysis, and inspect powder appearance before release.
12. Fire & Explosion Safety Procedure
If smoke or fire is detected, stop product feed, shut down burner, activate fire suppression system, and evacuate non-essential personnel.
13. Controlled Shutdown Procedure
1. Stop slurry feed gradually
2. Maintain airflow during cooldown
3. Reduce burner firing slowly
4. Allow chamber cooling
5. Clean powder collection system
14. Maintenance Inspection Checklist
Inspect burners, thermocouples, atomizers, blowers, cyclone separators, humidity sensors, and fire suppression systems.
15. Operator Logging Requirements
Operators must record alarm type, timestamp, sensor values, batch number, corrective actions, QA notifications, and shutdown actions.
16. Escalation Matrix
Warning Alarm → Shift Operator
Critical Alarm → Shift Supervisor
Powder Quality Risk → QA Department
Fire/Explosion Risk → Safety Team + Plant Manager
