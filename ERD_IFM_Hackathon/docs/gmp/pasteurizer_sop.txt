STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE: PASTEURISER CRITICAL CONTROL POINTS
Document: pasteurizer_sop.txt | Version: 1.0 | Effective: 2026-01-01
Applicable Standard: FDA 21 CFR Part 110, Codex Alimentarius CAC/RCP 1-1969

§1 Normal Operation
The pasteuriser must achieve and maintain all three CCP parameters simultaneously:
(a) Product temperature: minimum 72°C for a minimum hold time of 15 seconds (High Temperature
Short Time — HTST pasteurisation). (b) Product pH: 6.5 to 7.0. (c) Flow rate: minimum 80 L/h.
Any single parameter outside its CCP range constitutes a deviation requiring immediate action.

§2 Temperature Below Minimum (Below 72°C)
§2.1 Immediate Response
Activate the divert valve to redirect product flow back to the feed tank. Do not allow
sub-pasteurised product to pass forward in the process line. Acknowledge alert within 90
seconds. Inspect heating plates for fouling and check steam supply pressure.

§2.2 Product Disposition
All product in the hold tube at the time of deviation is non-conforming. Segregate and
quarantine as per HACCP general §3. Batch must not be released until root cause is identified,
corrective action is verified, and three consecutive readings confirm temperature ≥ 72°C.

§3 pH Deviation
§3.1 Low pH (Below 6.5)
Acidification of the product stream may indicate microbial contamination or ingredient
ratio error. Place batch on immediate hold. Notify QA. Sample product for microbiological
testing. Do not restart until pH is verified and root cause is resolved.

§3.2 High pH (Above 7.0)
Elevated pH may indicate alkaline contamination from cleaning chemicals. Check CIP rinse
validation records. Product on hold. Conduct chemical residue test. Notify food safety manager.

§4 Flow Rate Reduction (Below 80 L/h)
§4.1 Reduced flow increases product hold time beyond the HTST design envelope, risking
overprocessing and protein denaturation. Inspect pump and check for blockages in the flow
control valve. Log flow rate readings for the preceding 5 minutes.
§4.2 If flow rate cannot be restored above 80 L/h within 5 minutes, shut down the
pasteuriser and place all in-process product on hold pending engineering inspection.
