STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE: GENERAL HACCP DEVIATION RESPONSE AND CORRECTIVE ACTION
Document: haccp_general.txt | Version: 1.0 | Effective: 2026-01-01
Applicable Standards: Codex Alimentarius CAC/RCP 1-1969 Rev. 4-2003 §5 (HACCP),
FDA 21 CFR Part 110, ISO 22000:2018 §8.9

§1 CCP Deviation Response
Any deviation from a Critical Control Point (CCP) limit — whether at the boiler, pasteuriser,
or dryer — must trigger a structured response within 2 minutes of alert acknowledgement.
The response sequence is: (1) acknowledge alert, (2) identify affected product, (3) implement
immediate corrective action, (4) place affected batch on hold, (5) notify supervisor and QA.
No product that has passed through a CCP deviation window may be released without QA clearance.

§2 Corrective Action Steps
§2.1 Immediate Control Action
Restore the CCP parameter to within its specified limit using the device-specific SOP
(boiler_sop §2 or §3, pasteurizer_sop §2 through §4, dryer_sop §2 or §3). Document the
time of deviation onset, the measured parameter value, and the action taken.

§2.2 Product Segregation
All product produced or in-process during the deviation window must be physically separated
from conforming product and labelled as quarantined. The batch ID, deviation start time, and
end time must be recorded on the quarantine tag and in the electronic audit log.

§2.3 System Verification
After corrective action, verify that the CCP parameter is stable within its acceptable range
for a minimum of three consecutive monitoring intervals before resuming normal production.
Record the verification readings with timestamps in the batch record.

§2.4 Notification
If the deviation duration exceeds 10 minutes or if product disposition is uncertain, notify
the food safety manager and initiate a full HACCP investigation per §4.

§3 Batch Hold and Segregation
§3.1 A batch placed on hold must be stored under controlled conditions appropriate for the
product (refrigerated if applicable) in a clearly designated hold area, physically separated
from released product. Access to held product is restricted to QA personnel.

§3.2 Disposition decisions (release, rework, or destroy) must be made within 24 hours of the
hold being applied. The decision must be documented with the authorising QA officer's name
and the technical justification. No held product may be released by production staff alone.

§4 Root Cause Investigation
§4.1 Trigger: Any CCP deviation lasting more than 5 minutes, or any deviation that results
in product being placed on hold, must be subject to a formal root cause investigation.

§4.2 Investigation Process
The investigation must identify: (a) the equipment or process failure that caused the
deviation, (b) whether the monitoring system detected the deviation promptly, (c) whether
the corrective action was effective, and (d) whether a systemic change is required.

§4.3 Corrective Action Closure
Identified root causes must be addressed with documented corrective actions. Preventive
measures must be implemented and their effectiveness verified at the next production run.
Open investigations beyond 5 business days require escalation to the quality director.

§5 Verification and Record Keeping
§5.1 All CCP deviation records, corrective action reports, and product disposition decisions
are part of the immutable HACCP audit trail. Records must be retained for a minimum of 5 years
per FDA 21 CFR Part 110.93 and must be available for regulatory inspection within 60 minutes.

§5.2 Monthly review of all CCP deviation records is mandatory. Trends indicating recurring
deviations at a specific CCP must trigger a preventive action review and, if necessary,
a revision of the relevant device SOP and HACCP plan.
