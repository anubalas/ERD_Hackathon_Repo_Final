STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE: STEAM BOILER CRITICAL CONTROL POINTS
Document: boiler_sop.txt | Version: 1.0 | Effective: 2026-01-01
Applicable Standard: FDA 21 CFR Part 110, ISO 22000:2018

§1 Normal Operation
The steam boiler must maintain outlet temperature between 150°C and 170°C and operating
pressure between 4.0 bar and 7.0 bar at all times during active production. Readings outside
these ranges constitute a CCP deviation and must trigger immediate corrective action per §2
or §3. Readings are logged every 30 seconds via the automated monitoring system.

§2 Temperature Deviation Procedure
§2.1 Immediate Actions (Temperature Outside 150–170°C Range)
Upon detection of a temperature deviation, the operator must: (a) acknowledge the alert
within 2 minutes; (b) inspect the burner control valve and thermostat sensor; (c) reduce
steam demand by 20% if temperature exceeds 175°C; (d) increase fuel supply if temperature
drops below 145°C. All readings during the deviation window must be flagged for batch review.

§2.2 Investigation and Documentation
If the temperature remains outside the CCP range for more than 5 minutes, the batch must be
placed on hold per §4. The operator must document: time of deviation, measured temperature,
corrective action taken, and recovery time. A root cause investigation must be completed
within 24 hours per HACCP general procedure §4.

§3 Pressure Deviation Procedure
§3.1 Overpressure (Above 7.0 bar)
Activate pressure relief valve immediately. Reduce fuel input by 30%. If pressure exceeds
8.5 bar, initiate emergency shutdown per §4. Log event with timestamp and peak pressure.

§3.2 Low Pressure (Below 4.0 bar)
Inspect feed water pump and check for steam leaks. Increase fuel supply incrementally.
If pressure cannot be restored within 10 minutes, place batch on hold and notify supervisor.

§4 Emergency Shutdown
Trigger emergency shutdown when: (a) temperature exceeds 185°C, (b) pressure exceeds 9.0 bar,
(c) water level sensor fails. After shutdown, batch is automatically quarantined. No product
from the affected batch may be released without QA sign-off and corrective action report.
