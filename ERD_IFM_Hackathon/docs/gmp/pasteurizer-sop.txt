Pasteurizer Operations SOP
Infant Food Manufacturing Plant – IoT Monitoring Simulation
Equipment: Continuous HTST Pasteurizer System
Machine Type: pasteurizer
Monitoring Frequency: Every 5 seconds via IoT telemetry
1. Purpose
This SOP defines the operational response procedures for abnormal pasteurizer conditions detected through the IoT monitoring system.
2. Process Overview
The pasteurizer thermally processes liquid infant food mixtures to ensure microbial safety and regulatory compliance before downstream processing.
3. Critical Importance in Infant Food Manufacturing
Improper pasteurization may lead to microbial contamination, product recalls, nutritional degradation, and regulatory non-compliance.
4. IoT Parameters Monitored
Temperature: 68°C – 82°C | Abnormal: < 65°C or > 85°C
Flow Rate: 150 – 450 L/hr | Abnormal: > 500 L/hr
Holding Time: 15 – 25 sec | Abnormal: < 10 sec
5. Parameter Definitions
Temperature ensures microbial destruction.
Flow Rate affects residence time.
Holding Time ensures validated pathogen reduction.
6. Alarm Severity Levels
Advisory → Drifting parameter
Warning → Near threshold
Critical → Threshold exceeded
Emergency → Food safety risk
7. SOP – Low Temperature
Condition: Temperature < 65°C

Immediate Actions:
- Acknowledge alarm
- Divert product to rework tank
- Verify temperature readings
- Check steam/heating system
- Reduce product flow

Emergency Shutdown if temperature falls below 55°C.
8. SOP – High Temperature
Condition: Temperature > 85°C

Immediate Actions:
- Reduce heating input
- Verify sensors
- Inspect flow stability
- Notify QA

Shutdown if temperature exceeds 95°C.
9. SOP – Low Holding Time
Condition: Holding Time < 10 sec

Immediate Actions:
- Divert product immediately
- Reduce flow rate
- Verify holding tube integrity
- Check controller settings

Shutdown if holding time drops below 5 sec.
10. SOP – High Flow Rate
Condition: Flow Rate > 500 L/hr

Immediate Actions:
- Reduce pump speed
- Verify flow meter
- Inspect control loop

Shutdown if flow exceeds 650 L/hr.
11. Product Diversion Procedure
Whenever CCP conditions are violated, divert product, place batch on HOLD, notify QA, and document the deviation.
12. Controlled Shutdown Procedure
1. Stop product feed gradually
2. Divert remaining product
3. Reduce steam supply
4. Flush system
5. Stop pumps
6. Record shutdown reason
13. QA & Regulatory Requirements
All CCP deviations require QA review, batch traceability, corrective action documentation, and supervisor approval.
14. Maintenance Inspection Checklist
Inspect sensors, steam valves, heat exchangers, flow meters, holding tubes, diversion valves, and PLC logic.
15. Operator Logging Requirements
Operators must record alarm type, timestamp, sensor values, batch number, corrective actions, and QA notifications.
16. Escalation Matrix
Warning Alarm → Shift Operator
CCP Deviation → QA Department
Repeated Alarm → Maintenance Team
Emergency Shutdown → Plant Manager + Safety Team
